Discover the World of Terrariums

Terrariums are miniature, self-sustaining ecosystems housed in glass containers. They bring a touch of nature indoors, requiring minimal maintenance while adding a serene and beautiful element to any space.

Terrariums: The Perfect Indoor Office Plants

In today’s busy world, many of us spend long hours indoors—working in offices, sitting at desks, or in front of computer screens. Terrariums are a simple and beautiful way to bring nature into these spaces. Unlike traditional houseplants, they thrive under LED or artificial light, which makes them ideal for offices and homes with little or no natural sunlight.

A terrarium functions as a self-sustaining ecosystem: once sealed, moisture circulates inside the glass container. This means they require very little watering—sometimes only once every few months. The plants used are miniature, slow-growing varieties that stay compact, making them easy to manage without frequent trimming. Because everything is contained, there’s no soil mess, spills, or clutter—just a clean, green, low-maintenance garden in glass.

All of these qualities make terrariums the perfect choice for modern indoor spaces. They bring a calming, natural focal point to your desk or living area, letting you enjoy a piece of nature while you work or relax indoors.

Terrarium Layers (Bottom to Top):

  • LECA Balls – Expanded clay pebbles that provide drainage.
  • Mesh – Prevents soil from mixing with the drainage layer.
  • Charcoal – Filters impurities and keeps the terrarium fresh.
  • Substrate – A mix of orchid bark, worm castings, horticultural charcoal, and sphagnum moss, creating a rich growing medium.
  • Plants – The final layer where your greenery thrives.
